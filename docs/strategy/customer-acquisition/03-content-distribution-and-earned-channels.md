# Content Distribution And Earned Channels Strategy

## Objective

Use proof-driven content, community presence, and repeatable asset loops to create qualified demand
for Celstate without depending entirely on search rankings or paid spend.

## Strategic Thesis

Celstate should not run a generic content-marketing playbook. The strongest content for this product
is visual proof that transparency quality matters.

That means the content engine should be built around:

- examples that show native transparency working well,
- prompts that people can reuse,
- subject-specific workflow demonstrations,
- short-form assets that naturally spread in design and creator communities.

The content should make one point over and over in different contexts:

> Celstate creates assets that are already transparent and usable, instead of asking you to clean up
> an opaque image afterward.

## What This Vertical Is For

This vertical is the bridge between product proof and audience discovery. It is especially useful
before Celstate knows exactly which segment will become the strongest customer segment.

Content and earned channels can teach the team:

- which examples people actually share,
- which subjects earn engagement,
- which communities care enough to click through,
- which workflows sound painful enough that Celstate's promise feels valuable.

## Content Principles

## 1. Proof First

Every distribution asset should show something concrete:

- logo edges that stay crisp
- character hair or flame details that survive transparency
- sticker packs ready for use
- product cutouts that do not need cleanup

## 2. Reuse One Asset Across Multiple Channels

A good output should be repurposed into:

- a spoke-page proof image
- a short social post
- a prompt example card
- a community post
- a case-style workflow demo

## 3. Tie Content To Use Cases, Not Vague Inspiration

Instead of publishing broad "look what AI made" content, publish workflow-specific proof:

- transparent mascot logo for a local coffee shop brand concept
- transparent app icon exploration set
- transparent sticker pack for a merch drop
- transparent character portrait for a game overlay or stream asset

## Core Workstreams

## 1. Showcase Asset Engine

Create a repeatable internal process for generating and curating:

- subject-specific examples
- before/after style proof
- close-up edge inspections
- themed prompt packs

These assets should feed both the SEO pages and the distribution calendar.

## 2. Prompt And Example Library

Celstate should publish example prompts not as random inspiration but as useful starting points.

Good prompt-library categories:

- logo prompts
- icon prompts
- sticker prompts
- mascot prompts
- sprite prompts
- ecommerce product prompts

Each prompt should include:

- the prompt itself
- a small preview image
- what this prompt is good for
- a "use this in Celstate" CTA that deep-links into the app

## 3. Community Seeding

Celstate should test communities where transparent output is part of real work.

Potential communities:

- design and branding communities
- indie maker communities
- game-dev communities
- ecommerce and Shopify communities
- creator/streamer asset communities
- print-on-demand and sticker seller communities

The goal is not to blast links. The goal is to post useful proof, workflow breakdowns, and examples
that earn attention honestly.

## 4. Social Distribution

Short-form content should highlight one proof point at a time.

Useful formats:

- close-up edge inspection clips
- side-by-side "native transparency vs removal cleanup" visuals
- carousel of prompt -> result -> use case
- "three transparent mascot directions from one brief"
- "five transparent sticker prompts you can use right now"

## 5. Segment-Specific Workflow Posts

Once early evidence appears, create content loops by segment.

Examples:

- for designers: concept exploration and export-ready assets
- for ecommerce: transparent product imagery and merchandising assets
- for game developers: sprites, icons, UI items, character cutouts
- for creators: overlays, sticker-style packs, thumbnails, channel assets

## Recommended Channel Priorities

### Tier 1

- owned landing pages and asset showcases
- X or LinkedIn if the team can sustain consistent proof-based posting
- Reddit and niche communities if posts are genuinely useful

### Tier 2

- short-form video platforms once the team has a repeatable asset-production workflow
- maker and launch communities when there is a clearer story bundle to present

### Tier 3

- broader brand channels that need higher volume and a bigger audience strategy than Celstate needs
  right now

## Reusable Content Loops

Content should compound across channels.

### Loop A: SEO To Social

- generate proof assets for a subject page
- publish the subject page
- repurpose the prompts and examples into social proof posts
- use engagement to decide whether that subject deserves more page depth

### Loop B: Community To Product

- publish a practical example or workflow in a community
- send users to a pre-seeded app state rather than a blank app state
- observe activation and purchase behavior by source

### Loop C: Prompt Pack To Segment Insight

- create a prompt pack for a hypothesized segment
- distribute it in the segment's natural channels
- see which packs generate clicks, signups, and completed generations

## Metrics

Primary:

- sessions from earned channels
- signup rate by channel
- activation rate by channel
- assisted first purchases by channel

Secondary:

- engagement rate on proof posts
- prompt-pack click-through rate
- community post click-through rate
- subject-level traffic quality by source

## Risks

### Content Without Proof

If Celstate publishes generic AI content, it will be hard to stand out and easy to ignore.

### Community Spam

If posts are link-first rather than value-first, communities will reject them and the brand will look
cheap.

### Too Many Channels Too Early

Celstate should create one strong content loop and one or two channels that fit it. It should not try
to be everywhere at once.

## What Not To Do Yet

- Do not start a broad educational blog.
- Do not chase vanity follower growth.
- Do not create filler thought-leadership posts unrelated to transparent-background workflows.
- Do not scale video production before the core asset engine is working.

## Immediate Deliverables

1. Define the internal asset-production workflow for subject-specific proof images.
2. Build reusable prompt/example packs that can power both SEO pages and social posts.
3. Pick one or two earned channels to test with proof-driven content.
4. Ensure every distributed asset can hand off into a seeded product experience.
5. Use performance data to identify which communities and segments deserve deeper focus.
