# Programmatic SEO Strategy

## Objective

Build the primary organic acquisition engine for Celstate by publishing subject-specific,
commercial-intent landing pages that match how people search for transparent-background image
generation.

## Strategic Thesis

Celstate should treat programmatic SEO as its strongest near-term customer-acquisition bet because:

- The product solves a narrow, legible problem.
- Search intent around transparent images is often tool-selection intent, not passive education.
- The product has a real technical differentiator: native transparency instead of post-generation
  background removal.
- The site already uses server-rendered SvelteKit marketing pages, which makes crawlability and
  indexing much simpler than for a client-only app.

The goal is not to publish hundreds of pages. The goal is to publish the smallest set of pages that
proves Celstate can turn search demand into activated users and first purchases.

## Positioning for Search

Every page in this vertical should reinforce the same product truth:

> Celstate generates a transparent PNG with a real alpha channel from the start. There is no
> background-removal step after the fact.

That framing matters because Celstate should not compete as a generic "AI image generator." It wins
when the query includes transparent-background intent or when edge quality matters enough that
background-removal tools feel compromised.

## Primary Information Architecture

Use a hub-and-spoke model.

### Hub

- `/transparent-png-generator`

This page targets the broadest category term and explains the product category. It should act as
the canonical browseable entry point for subject-specific pages.

### Spokes

Pattern:

- `/transparent-[subject]-png-generator`

Initial Tier 1 subjects:

- logo
- icon
- sticker
- character
- mascot
- product
- app-icon
- game-sprite
- avatar
- ecommerce-product

Tier 1 is intentionally biased toward categories where transparent output is part of the job to be
done, not a cosmetic nice-to-have.

## Why These Subjects First

The first wave should emphasize subjects where one or more of the following are true:

- The user already thinks in PNG terms.
- Transparent output is required for the final workflow.
- Edge quality is easy to explain and visually prove.
- The query implies tool intent rather than casual inspiration intent.

That is why `logo`, `icon`, `sticker`, `mascot`, `sprite`, and `product` are stronger than vague
creative keywords like `art`, `drawing`, or `beautiful image`.

## What Makes These Pages Legitimate

Programmatic SEO becomes dangerous when pages are mostly the same and exist only to intercept query
 variations. To avoid low-value, doorway-like pages, every spoke must stand on its own.

Each page should include:

- A subject-specific headline and body copy.
- Five to ten prompt examples that are genuinely tailored to that subject.
- One to three first-party example outputs generated by Celstate.
- A section on why transparency quality matters for that subject.
- A short workflow explanation for how that asset is actually used.
- Subject-specific creation tips.
- A CTA that hands off into the app with a pre-seeded prompt.
- Links back to the hub and to two or three closely related spokes.

## Required Page Template

Every spoke should follow the same structural skeleton, but each section must contain subject-level
substance.

### 1. Hero

- Query-aligned title
- Sharp positioning statement
- Immediate CTA into the app
- One proof image above the fold

### 2. Why This Subject Is Hard

Examples:

- Logos: fine type, thin strokes, brand marks, export readiness.
- Characters: hair, fur, smoke, flame wisps, soft edge transitions.
- Product shots: shadows, glass, reflections, lighting cues, clean cutouts.
- Sprites: readable silhouettes, small-size clarity, aliasing risk.

### 3. Example Prompts

Prompts should not just vary nouns. They should model real intent.

Examples for logos:

- "Minimal veterinary clinic logo, flat vector look, transparent background, warm terracotta accent"
- "Esports dragon mascot logo, fierce expression, transparent background, bold outline"

### 4. Real Output Examples

Use first-party outputs generated with Celstate. Do not use stock art or mockups that weaken the
credibility of the page.

### 5. Why Native Transparency Wins

This is the strategic differentiation section. It should explain why generating with transparency is
better than generating first and removing later.

### 6. Best Practices for the Subject

This section should teach the user how to ask for a better result.

### 7. Related Generators

Cross-link related spokes with honest topical overlap.

### Reusable SEO Data Layer

Build this strategy on a structured content source, not on hard-coded one-off pages. At minimum,
the site should have:

- a subject registry
- prompt examples per subject
- proof assets per subject
- related-page relationships
- page copy fields

That lets Celstate ship high-quality pages fast without making future maintenance painful.

## Rollout Plan

Do not launch one page per day. Do not launch fifty pages in a single blind batch. Launch in waves.

### Wave 1

- Hub page
- 8 to 12 Tier 1 spokes

This is enough to create a real site section and gather indexing, CTR, signup, and activation data.

### Wave 2

Expand only after identifying:

- which pages index fastest
- which pages earn impressions for the intended query class
- which pages produce signup and first-generation behavior
- which subject clusters lead to first purchases

### Wave 3

Add adjacent page types only after spokes prove useful, including:

- comparison pages
- use-case pages
- prompt-library pages
- workflow pages by segment

## Measurement

Use this vertical to answer two separate questions.

### Traffic Questions

- Are the pages getting indexed?
- Which keyword clusters are earning impressions?
- Which pages get real CTR?

### Customer Questions

- Which pages drive signups?
- Which pages drive first-generation completion?
- Which pages eventually correlate with first purchases?

Subject-level attribution should be wired into the existing growth event system in [src/lib/analytics/growth-events.ts](file:///C:/Users/emrec/codebase/active-projects/celstate/src/lib/analytics/growth-events.ts#L1-L17).

Suggested dimensions to attach:

- landing page slug
- subject
- prompt seed used
- CTA id
- app entry path

## Success Metrics

Primary:

- indexed Tier 1 pages
- organic sessions to hub and spokes
- signup rate from SEO landers
- activation rate from SEO landers
- first purchase rate from SEO landers

Secondary:

- CTR by page cluster
- average time to first generation from SEO traffic
- percentage of users who use the seeded prompt as-is vs editing it

## Risks

### Thin or Doorway-Like Content

If pages are mostly interchangeable, Google may treat them as low-value scale content. The answer is
not slower publishing. The answer is stronger page substance.

### Weak Product Handoff

If Celstate gets impressions and clicks but drops users into a blank app state, SEO pages may fail
for conversion reasons rather than traffic reasons.

### Wrong Subject Priorities

The first batch is still a hypothesis. Some subjects will likely turn out to be more useful than
others. That is why the rollout must happen in waves with attribution.

## What Not To Do Yet

- Do not build hundreds of pages.
- Do not publish location pages.
- Do not start with generic glossary content.
- Do not depend on comparison pages before Celstate has stronger brand awareness.
- Do not write SEO pages that sound like background-removal pages with Celstate pasted in.

## Immediate Deliverables

1. Add a sitemap route and submit it once live.
2. Build a reusable hub-and-spoke page system backed by structured content data.
3. Implement app deep-link prompt seeding.
4. Launch the hub plus 8 to 12 Tier 1 spokes.
5. Review indexing and conversion data before expanding.
